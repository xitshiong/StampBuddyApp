# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two-sided marketplace — neither side works without the other.

**Customers**: Cafe regulars who are tired of losing paper loyalty cards. They want a frictionless way to collect stamps across multiple cafes and actually redeem rewards. Mobile-first, casual, not tech-savvy. They use this in the moment — standing at a counter, phone in hand.

**Merchants**: Small independent cafe owners who want a simple loyalty program without expensive POS integrations or monthly SaaS fees. They're time-poor, not technical, and skeptical of complexity. They need something that just works.

## Product Purpose

StampBuddy replaces paper loyalty cards with a digital wallet. Customers collect stamps via QR codes; merchants generate one-time QR sessions from a simple numpad. No app install required beyond a PWA. No hardware. No friction.

Success looks like: a customer walks into a cafe, the merchant taps a number and shows a QR, the customer scans it, stamps appear. Under 10 seconds.

## Brand Personality

Friendly, warm, accessible. Not a startup. Not a SaaS product. Feels like it was made by someone who actually goes to cafes.

Three words: **neighbourly, honest, warm**.

Emotional goal: customers feel like their loyalty is genuinely valued. Merchants feel like they finally have a tool that respects their time.

## Anti-references

- Startup landing page clichés: hero metrics, gradient text, glassmorphism cards, "10x your retention" copy
- Generic SaaS aesthetic: Inter font, blue accents, identical card grids, modal-first UX
- Cold fintech: dark navy, sharp edges, data-heavy dashboards
- Fast food app: bright primaries, cartoon icons, heavy drop shadows

## Design Principles

1. **Show the product, not the pitch.** The card stack IS the hero. Let the UI demonstrate the value before a word is read.
2. **Educate before converting.** Explain how it works for both sides before asking for a sign-up. Trust is earned, not assumed.
3. **Warm over slick.** Fraunces serif, amber tones, tactile textures. Feels like a neighbourhood cafe, not a VC-backed startup.
4. **Both sides matter equally.** The landing page must speak to customers AND merchants. Neither is an afterthought.
5. **Friction is the enemy.** Every screen should feel like the next step is obvious. No dead ends, no confusion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum. Colour contrast checked against the warm dark palette.
- Reduced motion respected (already in globals.css).
- Touch targets minimum 44px — this is a mobile-first PWA.
- No reliance on colour alone to convey meaning.
