# StampBuddy Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
**Customers**: Restaurant-goers across all dining contexts — from quick lunch spots to casual dinner cafes. Using the app in-moment at point of purchase: standing at counter, sitting at table, or on-the-go. Primary job: collect stamps quickly without friction, redeem rewards when earned.

**Merchants**: Restaurant staff (baristas, cashiers, servers) and owners. Using the app during service rush or between customers. Primary job: generate QR codes fast, manage loyalty program without slowing down service.

### Brand Personality
**Warm • Tactile • Effortless**

Emotional goals: 
- Customers: Delight in progress, satisfaction of completion, trust in simplicity
- Merchants: Confidence in reliability, pride in offering modern loyalty experience

### Aesthetic Direction
**Editorial Menu Board** — warm, physical, human-scaled. Think cafe chalkboards, printed menus, stamped loyalty cards you can hold.

**Visual tone**: Dark mode with warm amber/espresso palette. Asymmetric layouts, ruled dividers, numbered steps. Tactile depth through shadows and layering, not flat minimalism.

**Current foundation**: 
- Palette: "Midnight Espresso" (warm dark, not cold blue-black)
- Typography: Bricolage Grotesque (geometric sans with personality)
- Motion: Framer Motion for purposeful reveals and feedback

**Anti-references**: 
- Cold SaaS dashboards with blue accents
- Flat Material Design cards
- Generic fintech apps
- Overly technical/developer aesthetics

### Design Principles

1. **Physical First**: Design should feel like holding a real loyalty card — weight, texture, progression visible at a glance

2. **Speed Over Ceremony**: Every interaction optimized for service-rush context. No unnecessary steps, no modals unless critical.

3. **Warm Precision**: Dark doesn't mean cold. Amber accents, warm neutrals, soft shadows. Technical precision with human warmth.

4. **Progressive Clarity**: Start simple, reveal depth through interaction. Empty states teach, active states reward.

5. **Asymmetric Confidence**: Break grids intentionally. Varied spacing creates rhythm. Left-aligned layouts feel more editorial than centered.
